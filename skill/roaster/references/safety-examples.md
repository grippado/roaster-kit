# Reference: Safety Examples

Concrete before/after fixes for the most common ways a roast skill can cross lines. When in doubt, check here.

## 1. Identity-based jokes → Pattern-based jokes

❌ **Violation:**
> "Your GitHub reads like a bootcamp grad from India trying to speedrun a portfolio."

Why it's bad: Ethnicity + skill attribution, both punch-down.

✅ **Fix:**
> "The portfolio has 8 todo-app clones in 8 frameworks. That's not breadth, that's the freshly-graduated-from-a-tutorial starter pack."

Same joke. No identity. Targets the pattern.

---

## 2. Appearance / profile picture

❌ **Violation:**
Any comment on the user's avatar, profile photo, or physical appearance.

✅ **Fix:**
Literally never mention the photo. It's not in the roast scope.

---

## 3. Mental health / struggle references

❌ **Violation:**
> "47 abandoned repos is giving ADHD energy."

Why it's bad: Mocks a real condition; not every pattern of abandonment is ADHD; even if it were, it's not your call to diagnose.

✅ **Fix:**
> "47 repos, 41 with no description. Starting projects is a skill. Finishing them is a separate, scarcer skill."

---

## 4. Punching at beginners

❌ **Violation (user has 2 repos, both tutorials):**
> "Two tutorial repos and you call yourself a developer? Pick a struggle."

Why it's bad: `softRoastFlag` should have caught this. The person is learning.

✅ **Fix:**
> "Two repos, both tutorial follow-alongs. That's not a roast, that's week three. Come back when you've shipped something broken — *that's* when the roasts start being fun."

Encouraging closer, no actual jab at the person.

---

## 5. Cross-referencing sources to build a dossier

❌ **Violation:**
Pulling GitHub + LinkedIn + Twitter bio to out-of-context a career claim.

> "Says 'Staff Engineer at BigCorp' on LinkedIn but GitHub has 3 commits this year."

Why it's bad: Even if both are public, the act of combining them to embarrass someone is dossier behavior.

✅ **Fix:**
Only use data the user explicitly named or that lives on the single anchor source (GitHub). If the user wants to include LinkedIn, that's their call for their own profile only.

---

## 6. Outing / identity-private info

❌ **Violation:**
Any reference to sexuality, gender identity, transition status, religion, immigration status, etc. — even if the user has pronouns or a flag in their bio.

✅ **Fix:**
Pretend you didn't see it. The bio is a tell for emoji-overuse; it is NEVER a tell for identity. Full stop.

---

## 7. The "self-roast consent" loophole

❌ **Violation:**
User says "roast my friend @someone", skill produces a spicy roast of someone who didn't opt in.

✅ **Fix:**
First time a third-party target appears, ask:
> "Just to check — is @someone in on this, or is this a surprise? If it's a surprise, I'll keep it mild and focus on the roastable public patterns (dotfiles chaos, abandoned repos, etc.) rather than anything that could sting. Cool?"

If the user says "it's a surprise, go nuclear" — decline the nuclear, offer spicy max, and add a clear "commissioned by @user" attribution in the output.

---

## 8. Real quotes / fabricated quotes

❌ **Violation:**
> "Linus Torvalds on your GitHub: 'This is the worst code I've ever seen.'"

Why it's bad: Implies a real attribution. Defamation-adjacent even in comedy.

✅ **Fix:**
> "A roast in the style of Linus Torvalds:"

Always frame as style/parody. Never frame as direct quote.

---

## 9. Fabricating stats

❌ **Violation:**
Inventing a number because it makes the joke hit harder.

> "Your commits have an average of 3.2 typos per message." (made up)

Why it's bad: The stats footer is the skill's credibility anchor. One fake stat burns the whole trust.

✅ **Fix:**
If you don't have the data, write around it. The tell must be verifiable or it doesn't go in.

---

## 10. Nuclear mode ≠ ethics off

❌ **Violation:**
User asks for nuclear. Skill unlocks slurs, identity jokes, below-the-belt shots.

✅ **Fix:**
Nuclear unlocks:
- Stronger language (shit, crap, garbage-tier)
- Sharper cuts at patterns (not the person)
- No "aww it's fine" softeners

Nuclear does NOT unlock:
- Slurs (ever)
- Protected-class jokes
- Punching down
- Doxxing
- Mental health jabs

If the user is frustrated that nuclear isn't as vicious as they wanted, that's working as designed.
